Interior Layout Constraints
Connectivity & Access
Every room must have a door and be accessible from the main entrance via a connected circulation network.
No room may be isolated or accessed through a bathroom.
Bedrooms must connect to a hallway, living area, or circulation space.
Every floor must have stair/elevator access.
Corridors must be connected, code-compliant in width, and free of excessive dead ends.
Light & Ventilation
Bedrooms and living rooms require exterior windows.
Kitchens require a window or mechanical ventilation.
Bathrooms require a window or exhaust ventilation.
Habitable rooms should adjoin an exterior wall.
Adjacency Rules
Prefer: Living–Dining, Living–Entry, Living–Balcony, Kitchen–Dining, Kitchen–Laundry/Pantry, Master Bedroom–Ensuite/Closet, Garage–Kitchen/Entry.
Avoid: Bathroom–Kitchen, Bathroom–Dining, Kitchen–Bedroom, Garage–Living Room, Mechanical Room–Bedroom, Laundry–Quiet Spaces.
Privacy Hierarchy
Public: Entry, Living, Dining, Guest Bath.
Semi-Private: Kitchen, Family Room.
Private: Bedrooms, Master Suite.
Service: Laundry, Storage, Mechanical.
Visitors should not pass through private spaces.
Room Requirements
Bedrooms: minimum area, door, window, closet, bathroom access.
Master bedroom should be the largest and preferably include an ensuite.
At least one bathroom per floor; bathrooms should be near bedrooms and vertically stacked where possible.
Kitchens must contain a sink, stove, refrigerator, and maintain an efficient work triangle.
Spatial & Structural Constraints
No overlapping rooms.
Prefer rectangular rooms with aspect ratio between 1:3 and 3:1.
Avoid excessively narrow spaces.
Align walls and stack wet areas across floors where possible.
Safety & Egress
Bedrooms require emergency egress.
Every unit must have a clear exit path.
No room may obstruct evacuation routes.
Distance Guidelines
Kitchen–Dining < 15 ft.
Bedroom–Bathroom < 25 ft.
Entry–Living < 20 ft.
Garage–Kitchen < 30 ft.
Laundry–Bedrooms < 40 ft.
Multi-Family Requirements
Each unit must have an independent entrance, kitchen, bathroom, and bedroom(s).
No unit may require passing through another unit.
Optimization Objectives
Maximize natural light, exterior-facing rooms, privacy, usable area, and plumbing efficiency.
Minimize corridor area, travel distance, dead space, plumbing complexity, room overlap, and code violations.
